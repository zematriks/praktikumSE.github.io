OVERVIEW

The Persano horse, known as Reale Razza Di Persano, is a rare breed that originated in Italy, specifically established in the early 18th century by Charles III of Borbone. .

DESCRIPTION

Country of Origin   Italy
Weight   800 to 1,000 lb (360 to 450 kg)
Height   Persano horses typically stand between 15.2 to 16.2 hands high (62 to 66 inches at the shoulder).
Color   Bay, black, chestnut, Piebald, or Skewbald.

CHARACTERISTICS

HEAD

The head of a Persano horse is generally well-proportioned, with a refined profile. It often has a slightly convex shape, contributing to its noble appearance.


BUILD

They have a strong, muscular build with a deep chest, allowing for good stamina and strength. Their bodies are compact and well-structured, suitable for various riding disciplines.


TEMPERAMENT

Persano horses are known for their calm and docile temperament. They are generally willing and eager to work, making them suitable for various riders.


OVERALL

The Persano horse presents an elegant and graceful appearance, combining strength with beauty. Their well-balanced proportions and refined features make them stand out in the equestrian world.
